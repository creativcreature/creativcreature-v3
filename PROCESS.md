## Timeline

### Phase 0: Initial State Assessment
**Date:** 2026-02-01 ~12:00 GMT

**Problems identified:**
- Case studies looked "fake" — no cohesion, generic branding
- Missing images / empty placeholders
- No visual evidence in a Creative Director portfolio
- Fake brands lacked Fortune 500 quality execution

### Phase 1: Meta Hiring Manager Critique (MiloX)
**Round 1 Verdict:** IMMEDIATE REJECT

**Critical failures:**
1. No images anywhere
2. Fake case studies with no recognizable clients
3. Site looked broken (empty gray boxes)
4. Generic stats ("10+ years, 50+ projects")
5. No outcomes or metrics
6. No testimonials / social proof

**What Meta actually wants:**
- Real clients OR well-documented personal projects
- Before/after comparisons
- Specific metrics
- Process documentation
- Systems thinking

### Phase 2: Brand Development Methodology Research (Miloshh)
**Sources:** Pentagram, Wolff Olins, Fortune 500 case studies

**Methodology established for fake brands:**

Each fake brand needs a complete identity system:

1. **Logo System**
   - Primary mark
   - Secondary/simplified mark
   - Horizontal lockup
   - Icon for small applications
   - Clear space rules

2. **Color Palette** (5-7 max)
   - 2 Primary colors
   - 3-4 Secondary/support colors
   - 1-2 Accent colors
   - All with HEX codes

3. **Typography** (2 fonts max)
   - Headline font
   - Body font

4. **Application Mockups** (5-8 per brand)
   - Signage/environmental
   - Digital (website, app)
   - Print (business cards, letterhead)
   - Packaging (if relevant)
   - Social media

**Key insight from Pentagram:**
> "Without proper planning, it's very easy to create something that looks beautiful in your case study but doesn't really suit the brand when they come to using it on a daily basis."
